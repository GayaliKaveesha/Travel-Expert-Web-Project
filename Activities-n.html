THINGS TO DO IN SOUTHERN PROVINCE

Most Popular things to do

Mirissa Beach

Matara District, Southern Province

Mirissa is small town on south coast of Sri Lanka.
This place is the safest place for swimming. If you are a strong swimmer, you can swim past the rocks, all depending on how rough the ocean is.

things to do: plan a trip, surfing, see the secret beach

Galle Fort

Galle District, Southern Province

Galle Fort, in the Bay of Galle on the southwest coast of Sri Lanka, was built first in 1588 by the Portuguese, then extensively fortified by the Dutch during the 17th century from 1649 onwards.

hours: Open 24 hours

Diving at Unawatuna

Galle District, Southern Province

Diving in Unawatuna is a famous activity to do to discover the true beauty that exists in the southern seas in Sri Lanka.
Unawatuna is lined with palm trees and is reputedly one of the best beaches in the world.

To See Coral Reef in Hikkaduwa

Galle District, Southern Province

Hikkaduwa Coral Reef is one of the best Attractions located in Galle Hikkaduwa National Park is one of the three marine national parks in Sri Lanka.

Open hours: Everyday from 7.00am to 6.30pm

Whale Watching in Mirissa

Galle District, Southern Province

Whale watching in Mirissa is one of the most exciting water activities you can do during your holiday in Sri Lanka.

Whale watching season is from November to April.

Sinharaja Rain Forest

Galle District, Southern Province

Sinharaja Forest is of international significance and has been designated a Biosphere Reserve and World Heritage Site by UNESCO.

Open hours: Everyday from 6am to 6pm

Sithulpawwa Buddhist Temple

Hambantota District, Southern Province

Sithulpawwa Rajamaha Viharaya is an ancient Buddhist monastery.It is believed to have been built in the 2nd century B.C by king Kavantissa.

Open hours: Everydays in 24 hours

Snake Farm

Weherahena Buddhist Temple

Matara District, Southern Province

Known as the first tunnel temple in the world, the Weherahena Temple is a sacred place of worship that is has a touch of class which attracts many Buddhists to visit the place for it has a serene atmosphere.

Hambantota District, Southern Province

This springs are also known as Madunagala hot springs.
Temperature of each well is different the middle one being very hot and other going down to lukewarm to just warm.
